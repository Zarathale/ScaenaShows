THE ARC
Something is about to happen. The world has been preparing. The build is the preparation —
departments layering in, the Welcome stage awakening. Then: the player crosses a threshold
and the world responds — completely, and at once. The celebration that follows is real and
full, but it knows how to end. The Coda is one quiet thing. The player is left standing in
a world that now belongs to them.

THE PLAYER
The one arriving. The guest who becomes a resident. Not an audience member watching a
celebration — the center of it. The show knows they are here.

TONE
Warm, earned, celebratory — but not loud. A full-body hug, not a party horn. The difference
between "WELCOME!!!" and "You're here. Good."

WHAT THEY CARRY AWAY
You are here. This place knows you.

- Duration: compact — the show should not outstay its welcome after the welcome
- Structure: Build → Arrival → Celebration → Coda
- The Arrival is a single coordinated moment, not a sequence of responses
- Effects and Fireworks must agree on player altitude before any other dept authors
- Choreography: the welcoming committee arrives BEFORE the player — they are waiting
- The Welcome stage is permanent; Set designs for longevity, not just this show

## The Arrival — Coordination Contract

The Arrival is the most technically demanding moment in the Showcase Series. Every
department contributes to the same tick window. This section establishes the contract
before intake begins.

**What must be agreed before any department authors:**

1. **Altitude (Effects + Fireworks):** Where is the player at the moment of Arrival?
   This determines Mira's y_offsets for everything that fires. Effects and Fireworks
   agree on this number and hold it through all revisions unless the Director approves
   a change.

2. **Facing (Camera + Set):** Where is the player looking when Arrival fires? Mark and
   Set agree on the player's orientation relative to the Welcome stage's welcome axis.

3. **The Threshold (Set):** What is the player crossing? Set must define the physical
   or spatial marker of arrival — the line between "approaching" and "arrived." This
   might be a TP to a specific set position, a mark, an orientation change. The threshold
   must be legible to the player without being explained.

4. **The welcome words (Voice):** Sprite's line at the Arrival must be written and
   reviewed by the Director before any department authors around it. The words shape
   what the Arrival means. Everything else amplifies them.
