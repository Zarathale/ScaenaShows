# Voice — showcase.03 "Welcome"

## What This Department Serves

Voice is the narrative lead for this show. Sprite carries the show's emotional argument. If
the words don't land, the show doesn't land regardless of what else works. The Arrival line
and the Coda line are reviewed by the Director before YAML is written. Voice has final say on
the show's meaning.

This is a show about being seen. The player arrives and the world responds — but the response
only becomes a welcome when someone says so. That's Sprite's moment. Not a fanfare. Not an
announcement. The thing a person says when someone they were waiting for finally walks through
the door.

The show direction says: "The words at Arrival must address the player as a particular person
arriving in a particular place. If the line could appear at any show for any player, it is
not the line. Write the one that only belongs here."

---

## The Architecture

### Build — "The Overture"
Voice may speak one line that names nothing but sets the register. Optional.

If used: it is ambient, not plot. Not "something is about to happen." The condition of
expectation — the thing you'd say in a room that knows someone is coming. Under 40 characters.

Draft candidates:
- "Something is ready."
- "They're almost here."
- "The wait is almost over."

Or: silence. The welcoming committee's visible presence, waiting before the player arrives,
may be sufficient to set the register without narration.

Voice brings a yes/no recommendation to intake. The Build line is not required; it is only
valuable if it adds something the visual scene doesn't already provide.

### Arrival — "The Threshold"
This is the line. One or two sentences, maximum. The thing that makes the Arrival human.

The show direction is precise: "the thing a person says when someone they were waiting for
finally walks through the door. It is specific, quiet, and true."

The Arrival line fires at the moment of Arrival — coordinated with Effects, Mira, Sound,
and Camera. It arrives simultaneously with everything else. It does not explain the fireworks;
it does not describe the committee. It acknowledges the player.

**What "specific" means here:** The line must reference something about this server, this
place, or this moment that makes it belong here and nowhere else. "Welcome" alone is not the
line. "You made it" is closer but still generic. The line should feel like it was written for
the person walking through this specific threshold into this specific welcome area.

This requires knowing, before drafting, what the welcome area looks like (Set), what the
committee is (Casting), and what the altitude feels like (Effects). The Arrival line is
written last among Voice's contributions — after the rest of intake is complete.

**Draft pool (placeholder — final line written after intake):**
- "There you are."
- "We've been waiting."
- "You're exactly who we thought you'd be."
- "This place is yours now."
- "Right on time."

The final line is reviewed by the Director. It is probably one of these or something better.
The process is: draft five candidates at intake, Director chooses or asks for more, one is
locked.

### Celebration — "The Occasion"
No Sprite lines during the Celebration. The fireworks, the committee, the sound are doing
the work. Sprite understands restraint. The Celebration does not need words.

### Coda — "The After"
One quiet line. The show direction: "an exhale. Not a conclusion. Not a summary of what just
happened. The thing you say in the silence after."

The Coda line fires after Mira's last firework. Not at the same tick — give the last burst
a moment to settle (60–120 ticks of silence), then Sprite speaks.

**What the Coda line must do:**
- Feel like the thing the welcome was always pointing toward — the statement that only lands
  because of everything before it
- Not recap (no "that was a welcome")
- Not conclude artificially (no "and now you're home")
- Be true in the specific world the player is now standing in

Draft candidates:
- "Good. You're here."
- "This is yours now. All of it."
- "We hope you stay."
- "That's it. That's what we wanted to say."
- (silence — the line might be no words at all; Sprite's last act might be to say nothing
  and let the world be the answer. Discuss at intake.)

## BOSSBAR Architecture

One bossbar for the show. Options:

**Option A — Section-named:** "The Overture" / "The Threshold" / "The Occasion" / "The After."
These are the show's section names. The BOSSBAR marks where the player is in the arc.

**Option B — Single sustained bar titled with the show:** "Welcome" or no title, progress
filling from Build through Coda. The show as one continuous thing.

**Option C — No BOSSBAR during Build and Arrival.** Activate only at Celebration, or not at
all. Let the Arrival be purely audio-visual-voice; the BOSSBAR doesn't fire until the player
needs structural orientation.

Recommendation: Option A is the clearest structural support. Option C is the most ceremonially
pure. Discuss at intake.

## Show-Level Constraints

- **The Arrival line is Director-reviewed before YAML is written.** No exceptions.
- **The Coda line is Director-reviewed before YAML is written.** No exceptions.
- Sprite does not speak during the Celebration. The temptation will be strong; resist it.
  Trust that every other department is saying what needs to be said.
- The Arrival line fires simultaneously with the convergence — it is not delayed by 10 or
  20 ticks "to let the player process the effects first." The line is part of the convergence,
  not a response to it.
- The Coda line fires with silence on both sides. Before it: at least 60 ticks after the
  final firework. After it: the show ends without additional narration.

4. **BOSSBAR structure:** Option A, B, or C?
5. **Coda silence duration:** How many ticks between the last firework and the Coda line?
