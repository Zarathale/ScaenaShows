# Lighting — showcase.03 "Welcome"

## What This Department Serves

Lighting owns the atmospheric register across the show's four sections. The tone brief
describes the arc in four states: "the Build is warm, expectant — the state of a room that
knows someone is coming. The Arrival shifts that state — not dramatically, but meaningfully.
The Celebration is bright. The Coda returns to something warmer and quieter than the
Celebration but different from the Build — the light of after, not the light of before."

These four states are Lighting's primary exercise for this show. The shift from Build to
Coda is not a return to where you started — it's a different warmth, carrying what happened.

---

## Per-Section Direction

### Build — "The Overture"
**Time of day:** Evening, warm. Expectant. TIME_OF_DAY ~13,000–14,000. The same quality as
a room that knows someone is coming — not bright, not dark, but in a state of preparation.

The welcome area's sky should be visible (Set's design requirement), and in this light it
should feel like a sky worth arriving under. If the Welcome Stage is built with architectural
elements, evening light should make them read as inviting.

**WEATHER:** Clear. Nothing interferes with the approach. The build's atmosphere should be
clean and warm.

This is the light the welcoming committee is waiting in. It should feel like the right time
of day for this — not dramatic, but correct.

### Arrival — "The Threshold"
**The shift:** Not dramatic. Meaningful.

The Arrival fires everything simultaneously. Lighting's contribution is a perceptible but not
violent shift in the ambient state. Options:

**Option A — Time shift:** A small TIME_OF_DAY advance (300–600 units toward a warmer or
brighter state) at the exact Arrival tick. The world gets slightly lighter when the player
arrives. The shift is noticeable; it reads as "the world brightening to receive you."

**Option B — WEATHER clarification:** If any cloud is present in Build, WEATHER clear fires
at Arrival. The sky literally opens.

**Option C — Combination:** Both a small TIME_OF_DAY advance and a WEATHER clear if any
cloud was in the Build atmosphere.

The shift must be gentle enough not to distract from the convergence (fireworks, sound,
Sprite, Effects all happening simultaneously) but distinct enough to register. Lighting's
Arrival contribution should amplify without competing. Discuss at intake.

### Celebration — "The Occasion"
**Bright.** The show direction is unambiguous: "The Celebration is bright."

TIME_OF_DAY should be in or near the brightest warm state that serves the welcome's register —
approximately 6,000 (mid-morning light) or Lighting's chosen equivalent. The point: this
section should look different from the Build. Not nighttime, not twilight — day.

Mira's fireworks fire during the Celebration. Fireworks are more visually impactful in partial
darkness than in full daylight. Coordinate with Mira on the target TIME_OF_DAY — if "bright"
means fully daytime, her palette needs to compensate. If "bright" means early morning or late
afternoon (still some sky color), fireworks pop better. Agree at intake.

### Coda — "The After"
**Warmer and quieter than Celebration, but different from Build.**

The Coda's light is the light of after. Not the expectant warmth of the Build (which was
anticipation); not the brightness of the Celebration (which was joy). Something that has
settled.

TIME_OF_DAY: late afternoon or early evening — approximately 11,000–13,000, depending on
where Lighting started the Build. The direction is: back toward evening, but carrying what
happened in between. If the Build was at 13,000 and the Celebration was at 6,000, the Coda
at 11,000–12,000 reads as a different time of day than the Build while having a similar
quality of warmth. That distinction is the point.

The shift from Celebration to Coda is the show's second-most delicate transition (after the
Arrival). Lighting begins moving toward the Coda state before Mira's last firework — so
the light is already settling when the Celebration ends, not snapping into a new state.

## Show-Level Constraints

- TIME_OF_DAY is Lighting's instrument. No other department alters it.
- WEATHER is Lighting's instrument. No other department alters it.
- The Arrival's light shift fires simultaneously with the convergence. Coordinate with
  Stage Management on the exact Arrival tick.
- Lighting begins the Coda transition before Mira's last firework. Coordinate with Mira
  on approximately when that last burst fires.
- The Celebration's TIME_OF_DAY is discussed with Mira before either authors. Fireworks
  visibility depends on ambient light.

## Intake Questions for Lighting

2. **Arrival light shift:** Option A (TIME_OF_DAY advance), B (WEATHER clear), or C (both)?
   How large is the shift?
3. **Celebration TIME_OF_DAY:** What value produces "bright" that still works with Mira's
   fireworks? (Agree with Mira.)
4. **Coda TIME_OF_DAY:** What value produces "the light of after" — warmer and quieter
   than Celebration, distinct from Build?
5. **Coda transition start tick:** Approximately when does Lighting begin the Coda shift
   relative to Mira's final firework?
