# Camera Director — Archetype Sampler

## Brief Received

> Camera mode: player-free. No cinematic sequences. Key orientation beats: initial facing
> at show start only.

---

## Decisions

**Decision: Player-free camera throughout.** The player owns their own camera for the entire show. This is an intentional artistic choice: the player's autonomy over their gaze is part of the experience. They are not being shown the show — they are inside it. Directing their camera would make it a spectacle; leaving it free makes it an experience.

**Decision: No PLAYER_SPECTATE sequences.** Cinematic camera would break immersion and pull the player out of the aerial arc. The altitude staging (Choreography) is the equivalent of cinematic movement — the player is already "moving through a frame."

**Decision: No FACE events on player.** The player is not redirected at any point. What they choose to look at is part of their experience of the show.

**Decision: BOSSBAR provides structural orientation.** Since the camera isn't directing attention, the bossbar color and title gives the player a contextual anchor for each section — they know where they are without being told where to look.

---

## Notes

R6 feedback flagged "player often above effects rather than inside them" — this is a Choreography concern (altitude arc), not a Camera concern. The player-free camera means they can always look down to find effects they're above. If anything, the R7 redesign (player closer to ground in early sections, effects designed around them) addresses this from the spatial side rather than by adding camera control.

The Camera Director has no planned work for future revisions of this show.
