# Show Brief — showcase.01 "The Cabinet"

```
SHOW BRIEF
----------
Title (working):  The Cabinet
Show ID:          showcase.01
Duration (target): ~8,400–9,600 ticks (7–8 minutes)
Context:          Calibration Showcase. First of three. Educational focus: departments
                  finding individual voice in short, contained moments. Every vignette
                  is a self-contained exercise. No cumulative arc required.
Development stage: Brief
Show type:        Calibration (Showcase)

THE ARC
The player arrives at a home base where an Allay — and some of what it has collected —
waits. Over five vignettes across distinct spaces in the overworld, nether, and end, the
Allay leads the player through what it has found remarkable. The show has no single rising
action: it rises and falls five times, each time returning to the quiet of the home base.
It ends where it began. The world is stranger for having shown you what it contains.

THE PLAYER
A guest at a private collection. Not a student, not an audience member. Someone who was
specifically invited to see something. They are in good hands. The collection is genuine.

TONE
Quietly eccentric, wonder-forward. A museum that opened just for you, run by someone who
finds ordinary things genuinely remarkable — and has been keeping track of them.

WHAT THEY CARRY AWAY
The world contains more remarkable things than they realized. Someone has been paying
attention.

CONSTRAINTS
- Duration: soft target 7–8 minutes; Stage Management sets the YAML clock
- Structure: Rondo (A B A C A D A E A F A) — home base between every vignette
- Stages: 5 vignette stages (B–F) + home base must be scouted before YAML authoring begins
- Effects: stay within verified levitation lane (HOVER/CLIMB/RELEASE only)
- Cue library: all families in scope; favor atmos.*, mood.*, fx.*
- Vignettes: each has one primary thing it does — resist adding a second beat
- Alan notes: Stage scouting is prerequisite. Every stage goes in the Set dept registry.
              Fireworks have one dedicated vignette (E). REDSTONE integrated into vignette F.
              The comedy beat (F) should land through coordination, not explanation.
```
