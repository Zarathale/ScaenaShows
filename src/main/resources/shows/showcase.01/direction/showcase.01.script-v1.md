## Line Notes

**Highest-ambiguity lines** (most likely to recontextualize at the reveal):
- B.2: "you move too fast for heavy" — describes the Vindicator; the player is present
- D.2: "I want you moving — right until the end" — right until the end of *what*
- E.2: "I know what you walk toward" — the Vindicator walks toward battle
- F.1: "I kept coming back to this place. It answers something." — the show's most
  loaded single line; now the final expedition's opening. The Armorer has thought about
  this place more than any other. The player is standing in the place that prepared
  their opponent's weapon.

**Finale beat** (Option 4 — warm complicity):
- A-FINAL.2: "You've been good company" — first line explicitly addressed to the player
- A-FINAL.3: "I'll leave you two to it" — acknowledges both the Vindicator and the player;
  the Armorer steps back; the job is done

**"I'll already be there" / "I'll be there" / "Meet me there"** — departure lines that
establish the Armorer's pattern of traveling ahead of the player to each expedition site.
These support the staging direction (Armorer already on scene at arrival) and add to the
ambiguity: who is the Armorer expecting?

**F.3 — open question for Voice session:**
"Five. Let's go home." is the current placeholder. Direction has authorized full silence
in place of line 3 at Scene F — Voice proposes both options at intake. If silence: the
Armorer picks up the axe and the TP home fires without any line 3. The last thing the
player hears in an expedition scene is "one good edge."
