# Choreography — showcase.01 "Preparing for Battle"

## What This Department Serves

Choreography stages the companion and the Hero — placing them where they need to be
so the show's images work. This is a functional role, not a dramatic one. The companion
does not perform; they work. Choreography keeps them oriented toward their task and
in relationship to the armor stand when at home base.

The show has two distinct staging problems: the companion's presence across all scenes,
and the Hero's staging for the finale reveal.

---

## The Companion at Home Base

The companion is present at home base on every A-section arrival. Their position should
maintain a clear sightline between them and the armor stand — not standing in front of
it, not blocking it, but in visible relationship to it.

At show open: the companion is at home base, near the armor stand, doing something that
reads as "preparing to leave." Not performing. Not facing the player directly. Working.

At each return: the companion arrives, places the piece (the equipping beat is Stage
Management's sequence), and re-establishes their home base presence. Choreography
ensures the companion's default position after each return is the same — or close
enough that the armor stand is always the visual center, not the companion.

If the companion has a travel entity (llamas, if Casting goes the Wandering Trader
route), those entities also need home base staging. Choreography documents this.

---

## The Companion on Expedition

At each expedition site, the companion arrives, moves toward the armor piece's location,
collects, and the TP home fires. Choreography stages the companion's initial position
at each site — the first image the player sees when they arrive.

The staging goal per expedition: the companion should be visible and clearly purposeful
on arrival. Not standing at a distance from the piece. Not blocking the scenery. Near
the object of the expedition, oriented toward it.

Choreography is not needed mid-expedition in most scenes — the companion moves toward
the piece, and the TP home fires. No sustained mob choreography required unless Set's
location has a staging problem (terrain obstruction, mob AI interference).

---

## The Hero — Finale Staging

This is Choreography's most important task.

The Hero must be staged for their reveal. Their position at the moment of appearance
determines whether the reveal works. The requirements:
- The Hero is visible from the player's arrival facing at home base (Camera confirms
  the arrival facing; Stage Management confirms the reveal spawn position)
- The Hero is not obstructed by the companion or the armor stand
- The Hero's arrival direction — where they enter from — should feel like they came
  from somewhere, not like they were spawned in place

**Two staging approaches for Choreography to propose:**

**Option A: The Hero walks in.** The Hero spawns at a position slightly off the home
base center — an approach angle — and a brief path brings them to stand before the
armor stand. The armor stand is full; the Hero arrives to claim it. This staging makes
the reveal a movement, not just an appearance.

**Option B: The Hero appears standing.** The Hero spawns at the reveal position already
in full kit (via `SPAWN_ENTITY` with equipment populated). The moment of appearance IS
the reveal. No movement — just the image. This works if the spawn position is exactly
right and Camera has confirmed the player's sightline.

Choreography proposes both options to Direction. Stage Management and Casting co-decide
at intake. The approach depends on: the Hero mob's AI behavior, whether pathing is
reliable for Option A, and which reveal register feels right once the Hero is cast.

---

## What Choreography Does NOT Do

- Does not stage the companion to be expressive or performative
- Does not add mob movement that draws attention away from the armor stand at home base
- Does not try to make the weapon scene (F) more interesting through mob staging —
  that scene works through the location and the particle beat (if Effects confirms),
  not movement

The companion works; the show watches. Choreography enables the work to be visible.
