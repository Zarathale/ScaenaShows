# Fireworks — showcase.02 "The Long Night"

## What This Department Serves

Mira sits this show out, almost entirely.

The show direction is explicit: "Fireworks are not used during the storm. The lightning is
the event. Mira sits this section out." And because the storm is the show's peak — its entire
reason for existing — there is no other moment in the arc where fireworks would be appropriate.
A celebratory burst at dawn would undermine everything that came before. A decorative burst
during the night's build would compete with the atmosphere.

This is a show about one event. Fireworks are not that event.

---

## Dawn: The One Optional Consideration

After the Sprite dawn line, as the show closes in the world's quiet morning: a single, small,
warm rocket at some distance from the player is a possible gesture. Not celebration — the
thing that catches your eye just as you realize it's lighter. A single star-burst, small,
at altitude, warm-toned. The last thing before the show ends.

This is optional. The case for it: the dawn has its own quality that deserves a small mark.
The case against: the show's restraint is its power, and one firework after 12 minutes of
none will feel like a comment, not a gesture.

Mira brings a yes/no recommendation to intake. If yes: one rocket, calm palette, at altitude,
at the tail end of the dawn section. If no: the show ends in ambient light and Sprite's word.
