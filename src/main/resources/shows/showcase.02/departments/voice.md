# Voice — showcase.02 "The Long Night"

## What This Department Serves

Sprite speaks twice in this show. No more.

This is not a constraint to negotiate. The show direction is explicit: "If those two lines are
not strong enough to carry the show's meaning, the lines need revision. The solution is not
more lines." Voice carries the show's emotional argument in exactly two sentences — the one
the storm says, and the one the morning reveals.

The challenge is writing lines that earn their silence on both sides. A show that runs 10–12
minutes with almost no narration creates a specific pressure on the words that do appear.
They must be exactly right, or their weight is borrowed rather than earned.

---

## The Two Lines

### Line 1 — Section D, The Storm Breaks
**When:** At the LIGHTNING strike. On or very near the tick of the flash.

**What it must be:** The thing the night has been trying to say. Not a description of the
lightning — the player can see that. Not a warning — too late. The thing the storm says,
which is not about the storm.

Candidate register: an observation about the world that the storm has made true in a way that
nothing else could. A recognition of something. Not a reaction — a revelation.

**Draft pool (not commitments — refined at intake):**
- "Even the light holds its breath."
- "Everything was always going to end here."
- "Some things need to break."
- "It knows where you are."
- "Not afraid. Something else."

The line must be short — one sentence, under 50 characters. Sprite does not give speeches at
the LIGHTNING strike. The flash is the speech.

**Delivery note:** The MESSAGE fires close to the LIGHTNING tick. The player will have seen
the flash before the text appears. Sound's thunder follows. Voice's word is the third beat —
flash, thunder, meaning. Coordinate with Sound and Stage Management on the exact tick.

### Line 2 — Section A', Dawn
**When:** After the dawn is already becoming perceptible — not to announce it, but to name
what it means.

**What it must be:** The thing the morning reveals. Not "dawn has come" — the player can
see that. The thing that is different now because of what happened during the night. The line
should land because of everything that came before it, not in spite of it.

Candidate register: an observation that could only be true after having been here since dusk.
Something small. Something that sounds obvious and isn't.

**Draft pool:**
- "It was here the whole time."
- "Lighter than before. In every sense."
- "A night is just — a night. And then: a day."
- "Survived it. That's the word."
- "The world doesn't explain itself. It just — continues."

Same length constraint: short, one sentence. The dawn does not need commentary. Sprite says
the one thing and then the show ends in the world's ambient light.

## Voice Architecture for this Show

### BOSSBAR
One bossbar, sustained for the show's duration or activated per section. Options:

**Option A — Single show-long bar:** Title "The Long Night" or blank; progress fills as the
arc progresses from dusk to dawn. One continuous thread.

**Option B — Section-named bars:** Each scene has its own BOSSBAR title (e.g., "Dusk",
"The Night", "The Storm", "Aftermath", "Dawn"). Section-by-section. Provides structure
without narration.

Neither option uses MESSAGE except for the two Sprite lines. The BOSSBAR carries the
structural sense; Sprite carries the meaning.

Recommendation: Option B. The player needs section orientation in a 10-12 minute show
with no teleports. The BOSSBAR is the quiet chapter marker. Discuss at intake.

### MESSAGE (Sprite)
Two events:
1. Section D — the storm line
2. Section A' — the dawn line

That is all. No other MESSAGE events in this show.

### ACTION_BAR
Optional. A single ACTION_BAR event could carry an ambient environmental note at arrival
(one word or phrase that sets the space before anything has happened). If used, it fires
once at show open and is not repeated. This is not Sprite's voice — it's the space announcing
itself. Or: skip it entirely. Discuss at intake.

### TITLE
Not used. The LIGHTNING strike is the show's most dramatic moment; a TITLE would over-explain
it. The show's restraint is its power.

## Show-Level Constraints

- **Sprite speaks twice. No more.** If an in-game test suggests a third line is needed,
  bring it to the Director as a structural question — not a revision to the Voice file.
- Both lines are reviewed by the Director before YAML is written.
- Both lines fire around Sound events. Sound stops new events 30 ticks before each Sprite
  line. Voice coordinates the exact tick with Sound and Stage Management.
- Line 2 does not announce the dawn. It must not say "the morning has come" or any
  equivalent. The player knows. Sprite says what it means.
- Voice reads Lighting's arc and Sound's co-design plan before drafting final lines.
  The lines must feel inevitable within the show's actual arc.
